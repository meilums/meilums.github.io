How It Started...

Becoming a graduate student with the iSchool of the University of Missouri has been an exciting next step in my career path. Although I am transitioning into a new field from teaching secondary education, I believe my academic and professional experience thus far has prepared me to work in an archive. I will be blending my background in history with the inventory management skills I picked up as a bookseller, as well as the new skills and knowledge I am gaining as a student of library and information science.

Studying history as an undergrad student at CUNY Hunter College instilled in me an appreciation for primary artifacts and archival research. While I was enrolled at Hunter, I also worked at Strand Bookstore primarily working in inventory management. This kind of work involves materials organization, attention to detail, and use of a database to manage information. At the time, these two paths did not seem to go together, so when I left New York for Austin upon graduating I decided to go into teaching.

In the Region XIII Educator Certification Program I received hands-on experience creating lesson plans, adapting materials and resources for the curriculum, and teaching social studies in a public school classroom. While teaching social studies at Austin Can Academy, a public charter high school, I learned how to adapt information for students who had varying levels of knowledge of US history, who spoke English as a second language, or who struggled through reading and learning impairments. This kind of experience taught me to be mindful that a diverse population will have diverse information needs, and how to adapt to meet those needs. Moreover, virtual teaching during the COVID-19 pandemic added another layer to this as I had to navigate teaching to students whose technology skills were at varying levels of proficiency.
